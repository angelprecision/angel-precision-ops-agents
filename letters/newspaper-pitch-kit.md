# Newspaper & Local Publication Pitch Kit

## Target publications

### Turlock / Central Valley
- Turlock Journal
- Modesto Bee
- Central Valley Business Journal
- Stanislaus County Business Journal
- Valley community / chamber newsletters

### Bay Area / Tech
- The San Francisco Standard (tech + local founder)
- SF Business Times (founder profiles)
- Silicon Valley Business Journal (profile coverage)
- The Information (high bar — only pitch after traction)

### Trade / Operator
- Benzinga (guest columns)
- Finimize (newsletter feature)
- Hacker News (Show HN when dashboard is live)
- Substack Reads (once publication has 500+ readers)

## Two pitch angles

### Angle 1: "Central Valley founder building Bay Area-grade infrastructure"
Local paper hook. Story framing:
- Turlock-based founder
- Building enterprise-grade monitored execution system
- Not a trader — a systems builder
- Working from outside traditional tech hubs
- Emphasize: discipline, infrastructure, operator mindset

### Angle 2: "Why automated trading needs operator oversight"
Trade-pub hook. Story framing:
- Industry opinion piece
- Kill switches, rejection logic, monitoring
- Why full autonomy is wrong for retail automation
- You as a technical voice, not a promoter

## Pitch email template

```
Subject: Founder story — Central Valley operator building monitored trading infrastructure

Hi [Reporter],

I'm [Name], founder of Angel Precision, a monitored options execution
and risk-management system I'm building out of Turlock, California.

I'm not a trader selling picks. I'm building enterprise-grade infrastructure
focused on execution discipline, rejection logic, and operator oversight —
the kind of system usually reserved for institutional desks, built for
controlled retail use.

I thought this might fit [publication] because:
- Central Valley founder building outside traditional tech hubs
- A counter-narrative to the "trading guru" space
- Concrete technical angle: kill switches, monitoring, incident documentation
- Willing to show dashboards, incident logs, and operator reports

Happy to send a weekly operator letter I publish, a dashboard walkthrough,
or answer any questions.

Thanks,
[Name]
[Phone]
[Angel Precision — booking link]
```

## What to include with the pitch
- Two recent Operator's Letters (Substack links)
- Dashboard screenshot (sensitivity = sales-approved)
- One-paragraph founder bio
- One-paragraph company description
- Headshot (calm, professional, not hypey)

## What NOT to include
- Performance claims
- Returns
- Client testimonials about money
- Hype language
- Anything that sounds promotional instead of editorial

## Founder bio template (110 words)
```
[Name] is the founder of Angel Precision, a monitored options execution
and risk-management system built from Turlock, California. He leads
product architecture, execution engine development, and risk-framework
design, focusing on observability, rejection logic, and controlled deployment.
His work emphasizes operator oversight, incident documentation, and
compounding trust over time — a deliberate counter to the speculative
trading-guru space. Angel Precision is currently in controlled beta,
accepting limited interest from clients who want disciplined execution
infrastructure rather than signals or guaranteed-return promises.
```

## One-paragraph company description
```
Angel Precision is a Central Valley-founded, Bay Area-grade options
execution and risk-management system. The platform focuses on monitoring,

speculative products. The company is in controlled beta.
```
