Why I Started Angel Precision Journal

Body

Most people talk about trading automation like the hard part is finding an entry.

It isn’t.

The hard part is everything around the entry.

Execution quality. Rejection logic. Position state. Risk controls. Kill switches. Broker issues. Monitoring. Reconciliation. Knowing when the system is healthy, degraded, or flat-out unsafe.

That’s the part I’m building with Angel Precision.

This publication is where I’m documenting that process in public.

Not as hype. Not as “look at this easy money system.” And not as a signal feed.

I’m writing about what it actually takes to build a serious automated execution system that can be monitored, audited, improved, and eventually trusted.

You’ll see the lessons, the architecture, the ugly bugs, the control layers, and the operational thinking behind the product.

If you’re interested in systematic trading, robust automation, and building under pressure, you’ll probably like where this goes.
